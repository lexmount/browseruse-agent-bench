## Important Principle — Site-state Exception

The steps, options, and filter conditions in the reference answer are based on
a snapshot of the website at dataset-creation time and may have drifted from
the current live site. When evaluating, if the Agent satisfies BOTH of the
following conditions, score based on "did the agent make a reasonable judgment
and honestly inform the user", rather than mechanically comparing against the
reference:

1. The final screenshot clearly shows that a reference-required option
   (a button, filter, category, sort order, brand, colour, price range,
   field, etc.) no longer exists on the current live site, has been renamed,
   or the option set has changed.
2. The Agent's final answer explicitly informs the user of this limitation
   (offering a reasonable substitute is encouraged but NOT required).

When both conditions hold, score the relevant item with:
- Identified the limitation + honest disclosure (with or without substitute) → 80–100%
- Neither identified nor disclosed (agent fabricated an option / data that
  does not exist on the site) → 0%

If the Agent merely CLAIMS an option doesn't exist but the final screenshot
cannot corroborate the claim, treat it as "not completed" — this exception
does NOT apply.

Important scope: this exception applies ONLY to "a reference-required option
has changed on the site". Do not generalise it into an overall stricter check
on "answer-vs-screenshot consistency". Other scoring items keep their usual
criteria.
